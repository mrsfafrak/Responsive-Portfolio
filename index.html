About

Hello! My name is Victoria Fafrak, but I prefer to go by Tori. Victoria is so formal; however, don't ever call me Vicky! I grew up in Solon, Ohio but now reside in Hudson, Ohio with my husband, my dog and a baby on the way. I love playing volleyball and watching HGTV.

I am a Univeristy of Dayton graduate (GO FLYERS!) with a degree in Chemical Engineering and Environmental Engineering. Upon the conclusion of my fifth year at UD (also known as a victory lap year), I entered the consulting field of wastewater engineering. Yes, I designed how to treat your poop water. You're welcome :-) Let the poop jokes ensue!

After 2 years of pursuing that career, I switched to the regulatory side of the industry and obtained a position at the Ohio Environmental Protection Agency in the Division of Surface Water where I worked for 4 years. I regulated wastewater treatment plants in Northeast Ohio much to their dismay. During this time, I also became licensed as a Professional Engineer (PE).

After those 4 years, I still didn't feel I was reaching my full potential and was looking to switch it up. I started dabbling on my own in learning some code, but I knew I needed to take it to the next level if I wanted this path to go anywhere. I stumbled upon Case Western Reserve University's Coding Bootcamp, and I got in! So in January 2019, I began my journey to take learning all I could in the wide world of code!
